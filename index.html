Lina-Speech: Prefix-Free and Scalable Prompting for Text-to-Speeh Synthesis




    Abstract: Neural codec language models, built on transformer architecture, have revolutionized text-to-speech synthesis, excelling in voice cloning by treating it as a prefix continuation task. However, their limited context length limits its effectiveness to few samples of moderate length. As a result, adapting prosody, accent, or an adequate emotion from a short prefix remains challenging. Moreover, the quadratic complexity of self-attention limits inference throughput and long synthesis. We introduce Lina-Speech, a new TTS model: motivated by empirical observations, it replaces self-attention with Gated Linear Attention as a principled backbone, improving inference throughput while matching state-of-the-art performance. In addition we address the problem of multi-sample conditioning through initial-state tuning, leveraging the stateful property of recurrent architecture to enable comprehensive voice cloning. We demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach for controlling fine-grained aspect of the voice such as emotion and prosody in a flexible way. We will release our code and checkpoints.

    Note: All prompted speakers and styles are unseen during training.
