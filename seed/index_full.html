PSW-Speech demo on Very-Long LibriSpeech

We propose simple adaptations to a standard encoder–decoder Transformer TTS architecture that enable streaming and long-form generation without limits on total duration. A prefixed sliding window governs autoregressive decoding; a sliding cross-attention window handles text conditioning. Trained exclusively on segments shorter than 30 seconds, our system produces seamless, consistent speech at practically unbounded lengths.

The first 8 seconds are the prefix. Token highlights track where cross-attention concentrates at each decoding step. While our ablations confirm that sink tokens are not necessary for synthesis quality, we retain them in this visualization as they give rise to a distinctive and interpretable attention signature.
